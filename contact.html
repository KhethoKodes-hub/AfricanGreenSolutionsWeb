Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about our services and contact process

How quickly can you respond to my inquiry? +

We typically respond to all inquiries within 24 hours during business days. For urgent matters or emergency service requests, we provide 24/7 support and aim to respond within 2 hours. You can call our emergency line at 083 892 4162 for immediate assistance.

Do you offer free consultations or site assessments? +

Yes, we offer complimentary initial consultations and site assessments for qualified projects in our service areas (Gauteng and surrounding provinces). During the assessment, we'll evaluate your waste streams, energy needs, and site conditions to provide preliminary recommendations.

What information should I prepare before contacting you? +

To help us provide the most accurate advice, please have the following information ready: type and quantity of organic waste available, current energy consumption or requirements, available space for installation, budget considerations, and any specific goals or challenges you're facing.

What is your service area? +

We primarily serve Gauteng and surrounding provinces including Limpopo, Mpumalanga, and North West. For larger projects, we may consider installations in other parts of South Africa. Contact us to discuss your specific location and project requirements.

Do you work with residential clients or only commercial? +

We serve both residential and commercial clients. Our solutions range from small-scale household systems to large industrial facilities. We'll design a system that matches your specific needs, whether you're a homeowner looking to reduce energy costs or a business seeking sustainable waste management solutions.

What is the typical timeline for a biogas project? +

Project timelines vary based on scale and complexity. Small residential systems can be installed in 1-2 weeks, while larger commercial projects typically take 4-12 weeks from initial assessment to commissioning. This includes feasibility studies, design, permitting, installation, and commissioning phases.
